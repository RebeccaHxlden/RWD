    The Life, Death and Exorcism of Anneliese Michel

    Published on 13 November 2019 by Rebecca Holden

    1 Minute Read

    • Anna Elisbeth Michel was born on September 21, 1952 in the small town of Leiblfing, Barvaria, Germany.

    • Michel underwent numerous Catholic exorcism rites, with priest Ernst Alt believing she was suffering from a demonic possession rather than her diagnosis of epileptic psychoses.

    • She died during an exorcism on July 1st, 1976, from malnutrition and dehydration due to her being in a partial-starvation state for almost a year.

    • Michel died weighing 68 pounds, her knees were broken and was reported to have pneumonia.

    EARLY LIFE AND HEALTH DECLINE

    Anneliese was raised a strict catholic and grew up being an obedient young woman described as bright and likeable, who showed her devotion to Christ.


    In September 1968, whenever Anneliese was 16 years old, she experienced her first time of losing consciousness. Almost a year later, in August 1969, Annelise experienced another event of losing consciousness.


    Her mother then took her to see the family doctor and a neurologist who hypothesized she was having a form of seizure and would later be prescribed an anticonvulsant and an anti-seizure medication.


    START OF THE SUPERNATURAL

    In the Spring of 1973, Annelise began hearing knocking sounds in her bedroom, these sounds would also be heard by her sister.


    She would also begin hearing voices damning her to hell and furthermore her mother grew to be troubled whenever she witnessed Annelise staring angrily at a statue of Virgin Mary with “Eyes turned black, jet black and her hands seemed to turn into thick paws with claws.”


    Annelise would later tell a neurologist she experienced horrific demonic visions of faces tormenting her, stated that she felt “the devil was inside her”, and reported smelling burnt faeces.


    In Autumn of 1973, she met with a Freudian psychiatrist and was diagnosed with neurotic with possible epilepsy, and another neurologist reported finding she had “epileptic patterns.” They then switched her medication to a much stronger anti-seizure medication.


    In the Summer of 1975, her condition worsened with her praying all throughout the night and eating spiders and flies. People around her reported her having “superhuman strength” and was seen squeezing an apple with one hand until “fragments exploded throughout the room”.


    EXORCISMS AND DEATH

    On September 24th, 1975 the first exorcism was held, and Father Renz allowed for some of the exorcisms to be recorded.


    Then in May her condition further worsened with her biting herself, banging her head against the wall, refusing to eat saying she was “not permitted to eat”.


    On June 30th, 1976 she had her last exorcism, saying only “Please, absolution” the entire time.


    The next day her family went to check on her and found her dead. She died of starvation and dehydration at the age of 23 after 67 exorcisms weighing 68 pounds.
