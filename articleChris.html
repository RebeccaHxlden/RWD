    The Chilling Case of Chris Watts

    Published on 13 November 2019 by Rebecca Holden

    1 Minute Read

    •Chris Watts of North Carolina murdered his wife, Shan'ann, and two children, CeCe and Bella.

    •Watts stated he murdered his family to be with his mistress of two month, Nichol Kessinger.

    Background

    Christopher and Shan'ann Watts were natives of Spring Lake and Aberdeen, North Carolina. They met in 2010 and were married on November 3, 2012. They then went on to have two daughters, Bella Marie Watts, aged 4, and Celeste Cathryn "Ce-Ce" Watts, aged 3.


    Murders

    The murders occurred on the morning of August 13, 2018. While being interviewed by police, Christopher Lee Watts admitted to killing his pregnant wife Shan'ann.


    Their daughters died by asphyxiation due to smothering. He then disposed of his daughters' bodies in oil tanks and buried his wife in a shallow grave at his work-site. At the time of Shan’ann’s death, she was fifteen weeks pregnant with a child which they planned to name Niko Watts; her due date was January 31st, 2019.


    The CeCe and Bella walked in instants after he had just murdered Shan’ann to which they saw “her eyes filled with blood”-due to the strangulation-according to Watts. He attempted to murder the girls, but they did not die. He then bundled them into his truck with Shan’ann’s body and their favorite blankets.


    In the truck, he covered CeCe’s face with her blanket and blocked her mouth with his hand. Bella sat beside CeCe as she was murdered. He put CeCe in the oil tank, to which he describes hearing “a splash as her body hit the oil.” He then went back down to Bella who asked, “What happened to CeCe?”, then he murdered her the same way and dumped her body in the oil tank.


    Watts has claimed he carried out the murders so he could be with his mistress of two months, Nichol Kessinger.


    Court Trial

    Watts pleaded guilty on November 6, 2018, to multiple counts of first-degree murder as part of a plea deal when the death penalty was removed from sentencing. He was sentenced to five life sentences without the possibility of parole, three to be served consecutively and two to be served.
