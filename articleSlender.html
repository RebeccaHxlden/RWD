    The Slender-Man Stabbing

    Published on 13 November 2019 by Rebecca Holden

    1 Minute Read

    •On May 31, 2014, two 12-year-old children persuaded their friend into the woods for a game of hide and seek, then stabbed her 19 times in an attempt to satisfy ‘Slender Man’ so they could become proxies.

    •The victim (Payton Leutner) crawled out onto a road where she was found by a cyclist; she recovered after six days in the hospital.

    •The perpetrators were found not guilty by mental disease or defect and, by February 2018, were sentenced to long periods in mental health institutions.

    Background

    Slender Man is a fictional creature who is tall, thin with a white face with no facial features. He is often depicted wearing a black suit but can morph to have tentacles. He was created online in 2009 for a Photoshop contest on a forum called “Something Awful”.  He is usually depicted in a forest, stalking children.


    Attack

    The perpetrators of the crime were 12-year-old Anisa Weier and Morgan Geyser. They initially planned to tape the victim’s, Payton Leutner’s, mouth, stab her in the neck and then flee-however they did not go through with this attack.


    The real attack occurred in a forest (David’s Park, Wisconsin) during a game of hide-and-seek on May 31st, 2014. They pinned the victim down and stabbed her 19 times in the legs, arms, and chest using a kitchen knife.


    The victim’s wounds were in two major organs, one missed the major artery of her heart and another went through her diaphragm which cut her liver and stomach. Anisa told Payton to lie down to slow the bleeding and said they were going to get help, but they never did.


    Payton then dragged herself to a road where a cyclist, Greg Steinberg, found her and called the police.


    Aftermath

    Anisa and Morgan were later apprehended, with the knife they used found in a backpack they carried. Morgan stated feeling no empathy at all, while Anisa was remorseful but felt it was necessary to appease Slender Man.


    Payton recovered in hospital and was released 7 days after the attack, and then went on to return to school a few months later.
